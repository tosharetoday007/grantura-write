Abstract
Type 2 diabetes mellitus is a major global health challenge, characterised by insulin resistance and progressive beta-cell dysfunction. This systematic review synthesises evidence on metabolic adaptation following dietary and lifestyle interventions, drawing on six key references identified through structured literature search.
Introduction
Type 2 diabetes mellitus represents a growing global health burden, affecting over 500 million individuals worldwide [1]. The disease is characterised by progressive insulin resistance and beta-cell dysfunction, leading to chronic hyperglycaemia [2]. Understanding the mechanisms of metabolic adaptation is essential for developing effective interventions.
Methods
A systematic search was conducted across PubMed, Scopus, and Web of Science for studies published between 2010 and 2024, following PRISMA 2020 guidelines [3]. Eligible studies included randomised controlled trials and cohort studies examining metabolic adaptation in adults with confirmed type 2 diabetes.
Results
Caloric restriction significantly improved glycaemic control in 78% of included studies [4]. Mitochondrial biogenesis was identified as a potential mediator of metabolic recovery, though evidence remains limited [5].
Discussion
These findings support the hypothesis that dietary intervention can partially reverse metabolic dysfunction in type 2 diabetes [4]. Further longitudinal studies are required to establish causality and long-term outcomes [6].
